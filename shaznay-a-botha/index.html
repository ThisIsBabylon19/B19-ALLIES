Count The Damsels In The Myths, Count Their Names, Janes Or Judies, In Those Tales Of Timeless Sense. Weight The Feminine Balance, The Wit Of The Cast Chalice, Amidst Artemis And Ada. Does Human Fancy Reflect Strength And Glory Of The 'she'? Have Women Lifted Time's Veil To Revive Our History? Call Them 'figures Of Purple,' Call The Known And The Unknown, Until Names And Faces Found. Watch Them Walk On Blue Water, Bend Winds, And Beasts Of The Sea, In Hegemonic Power.
Praise Us! We, Daughters Of Eve! Mother Genesis Gave All The Power Of Creation. And She, Bled With The Full Moon, Then She, Breathed Giants To Earth. Not Even Prometheus Could. Not Even Sisyphus Would. Cadet, My Dear Cadet, The Phallus Never Thanked Us! Your Duty, Here And Beyond, To Honor Madam And Eve, Is To Wake And Bravely Aim For The Light And Distant Sky. All That It Covers Is Yours. Matriarchy's True Blessing.
Mother Is Always With You! I Am In Your Laughters. I Am The Morning Rainbow. My Voice As One With The Wind. Echoes Through The Horizon, Touching All That Is Alive. Hear Words Through The Breeze; Feel Them! Embrace The Echoes Within, The Mystery Of Mother. I Am In Every Mirror. I Am The Gleam In The Sky. And If You Seek Me, I'll Be Humming Songs Under The Trees. Watch The Leaves Dance Around You. Cadet, My Dear Cadet, I Am Forever With You!
Some Time Ago You Were One. Eyes Wide Agape On The World, Knowing The Stadium Was Yours. Some Yesterday You Were Bold. All Nations Made Purple Rings; Eve By Your Side, You Conquered! Unity, Femineity, The World As The World Should Be. Some Time Today You Were Old. Pride In Your Heart, Majesty! You, The Pillar And The Glue. Some Eons Of Age To Go. Timelessly In The Fables, The Legend Of You Remains. Justice Was You, Fair And True; The Woman Manhood Should Be.
We Are The Light We Carry, As Bright As Ten Thousand Suns. Dear Cadet, We Are Who We Waited For, The Promise In The Mirror. And From The Ends Of The World, Cadet, Beloved Cadet, Hear Me: Darkness Decorates. We Are The World That Must Be, The Future Saving The Past. Dear Cadet, We Are The Titans They Fear, Multiplying The Purple. Cadet, My Dear Cadet, History Will Remember Valor Has A Name: Mother!
You Hold Heaven In Your Heart, A World Of Purple And Bliss, Dreams And Visions To Mold Out. You, Cadet, You! The Writer! You, Cadet! Divinity! You Hold The Sun By Its Light. Radiance, Majesty, Fire. Dark Is The World Without You. Hold My Hand, Cadet! I, Here! I, The Mother! I, Will Help! I'm Here For The World To Be. I Bring Whispers From Venus. “ave, Mother!”, Echoes Chant. Cadet, Oh My Dear Cadet, Purple Paint And Progeny Crowned You, Creator Of Truth.

Poet

Shaznay A. Botha

  1. 1. What do you believe is the impact of your words on the feminist movement?

    "I firmly believe that the impact of my words on the feminist movement is rather significant. Much like the age-old adage suggests, 'the pen is mightier than the sword,' and through my writing, I strive to make a meaningful difference. My words serve as a means to illuminate perspectives and dimensions of feminism that people may not have previously considered. Whether it's delving into the historical roots, examining the contemporary landscape, or envisioning its future, my writing endeavors to spark thoughtful contemplation and meaningful dialogue.

    In the historical context, my writing serves as a platform to amplify the stories of remarkable women who have played pivotal roles in advancing gender equality. Their unwavering determination and resilience, often in the face of formidable obstacles, underscore the enduring power of the feminist movement. By sharing their narratives, my aim is to pay homage to their enduring legacies and to inspire others to join the ongoing struggle for women's rights.

    In the present, my words function as a reflective mirror, offering insights into both the progress we have achieved and the persisting challenges within the feminist movement. They invite readers to engage with the complexities of feminism, encouraging introspection and a deeper understanding of the issues at hand. My writing endeavors to cultivate a more inclusive and equitable society by fostering open dialogue and critical thinking. As I look to the future, I envision my words as catalysts for change, shaping attitudes and beliefs, and contributing to a world where feminism is not merely a concept but a lived reality for all." -- SAB

  2. 2. How do you balance the need for artistic expression with the responsibility of being a feminist advocate through your words?

    "I've always found myself naturally inclined towards embracing diversity, variety, inclusion, and equality. These values have been central to my creative journey as a poet. I often find myself drawn to the interplay of ideas and concepts from a multitude of fields, as I firmly believe that they are interconnected in ways that might not always be immediately apparent. Therefore, my creative expression is deeply rooted in the need for inclusivity, and there are few instances where I feel that I am straying from the feminist and inclusive perspectives that guide my writing.

    In my poetry, I see a beautiful tapestry woven from threads of different ideas and experiences. It's a reflection of the belief that feminism and inclusivity aren't separate realms but are intricately linked with every aspect of our lives. Through my words, I seek to highlight these connections, often portraying them in unconventional and thought-provoking ways. In this process, I find a balance between artistic expression and the responsibility of being a feminist advocate, as my art itself becomes a tool for advocating for equality and embracing the rich tapestry of human experiences.

    Ultimately, my poetry becomes a medium through which I can actively engage with feminist and inclusive values. It's a means of not only expressing my creativity but also contributing to important dialogues and advocating for positive change. In doing so, I honor the essence of artistic expression while remaining true to my role as a feminist advocate, ensuring that my words carry the weight of both artistic integrity and social responsibility." -- SAB

  3. 3. Could you recommend a piece of your own poetry that encapsulates the essense of your feminist beliefs?

    "Allow me to introduce a piece of my poetry that I believe encapsulates the core of my feminist beliefs. It bears the title 'By your own definition.' This poem holds a special place in my heart as it delves into the realms of post-colonial feminism and intersectional feminism, which have long fascinated me.

    In 'By your own definition,' I explore the intricate web of post-colonial feminism, delving into the complexities of gender dynamics in a world marked by the legacy of colonialism. The poem weaves together various narratives and experiences, shedding light on the multifaceted nature of feminism in a global context. It invites readers to reflect on how power structures, cultural norms, and historical legacies intersect with gender issues, emphasizing the importance of an inclusive and intersectional approach to feminism.

    Furthermore, the poem underscores the significance of self-definition in the feminist journey. It encourages individuals to define feminism on their terms, recognizing that feminism is not a one-size-fits-all concept but a dynamic and evolving movement. 'By your own definition' serves as a testament to my commitment to exploring diverse facets of feminism through my poetry, striving to promote understanding, inclusivity, and the empowerment of all voices in the feminist discourse.

    " -- SAB
